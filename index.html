A grounded, collaborative approach

When being “fine” still feels exhausting

Many of my clients look put-together and “functional” from the outside, while internally they feel wired, tense, and tired from constantly managing anxiety, past experiences, or high pressure. They’re used to pushing through, but it’s no longer sustainable.

In our work together, I take a warm, structured, and thoughtful approach. We’ll make space for reflection and depth, while also using evidence-based tools to address anxiety, trauma, and burnout. I integrate CBT, EMDR-informed work, mindfulness, and body-oriented strategies to help you feel more regulated—not just in session, but in your daily life.

Signs it may be time to reach out

Therapy can help if you’re “getting by,” but the effort to keep everything together feels heavier than it should.

  • Persistent worry, overthinking, or a sense of always being “on alert”
  • Tightness in your body, difficulty sleeping, or feeling wired and exhausted at the same time
  • Old experiences resurfacing in relationships, self-doubt, or a lack of safety and trust
  • Pressure to perform, achieve, or hold it all together—even when you’re burnt out
  • Knowing something needs to change, but not feeling sure where to start

Our work will focus on helping you feel steadier and more resourced in your day-to-day life, not just in the therapy room.

Meet Dr. Reynolds

A calm, thoughtful space to reconnect with yourself

I’m a licensed clinical psychologist based in Santa Monica, California. I work with adults who are carrying a lot—anxiety, trauma, burnout, and the quiet pressure to keep functioning even when they feel overwhelmed on the inside.

My style is warm, collaborative, and grounded. Sessions are structured enough to feel supportive, while still leaving room for reflection and depth. I integrate cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), EMDR-informed approaches, mindfulness-based practices, and body-oriented techniques so we can address both the emotional and physiological sides of what you’re experiencing.

Trauma work is an important part of my practice. Whether you’re navigating a single incident or long-standing patterns rooted in earlier experiences, we’ll move at a pace that centers safety and regulation. My hope is that therapy not only helps with symptoms, but deepens your resilience and your relationship with yourself over time.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

Do you offer in-person or online sessions?

I see adults in-person at my office in Santa Monica, California, and I also offer secure telehealth sessions for clients located anywhere in the state of California. We can discuss which format feels most supportive for you right now, and you’re welcome to shift between in-person and online as needed.

What kinds of concerns do you specialize in?

My work often focuses on anxiety, panic, trauma, and burnout. I frequently support high-achieving, thoughtful adults who feel “functional” on the outside while struggling with worry, hypervigilance, tension in their body, trouble sleeping, or the ongoing impact of earlier experiences on their relationships and confidence.

What approaches do you use in therapy?

I integrate several evidence-based approaches, including cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), EMDR-informed work, mindfulness-based practices, and body-oriented techniques. This allows us to pay attention to both your thoughts and the way your nervous system is responding, and to tailor our work to what you need at a given time.

What can I expect from our first few sessions?

Early sessions focus on getting to know you and what brings you in now. We’ll talk about your history, current stressors, and what you’d like to be different, while also paying attention to how things feel in your body as we talk. From there, we’ll collaborate on a plan that balances practical tools with deeper exploration, so therapy feels both supportive and meaningful.
